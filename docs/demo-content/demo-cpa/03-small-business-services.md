## Entity Selection & Formation

If you're starting a business, choosing the right entity structure has significant tax and legal implications. We advise on:

**Sole Proprietor:** Simplest structure, no separate entity. Business income and expenses flow directly to your personal return (Schedule C). No liability protection.

**Single-Member LLC:** Adds liability protection; taxed the same as sole prop by default (Schedule C). Simple and cost-effective for most early-stage businesses.

**Multi-Member LLC:** Taxed as a partnership (Form 1065) by default; can elect S-Corp treatment.

**S-Corporation:** Popular for profitable businesses (typically over $50,000–$80,000 net profit). Allows the owner to split income between salary (subject to payroll taxes) and distributions (not subject to payroll taxes), reducing self-employment tax. Requires payroll, quarterly reports, and an annual corporate return.

**C-Corporation:** Separate tax entity with its own tax rate (21% flat). Appropriate for businesses seeking outside investment or planning to retain earnings. Double taxation of dividends is a key consideration.

**Greenfield CPA can help you:** Compare structures, model the tax impact at various income levels, and work with your attorney on formation documents.

## Tax Preparation

We prepare returns for all entity types:

| Return | Who Files It |
|---|---|
| Schedule C (Form 1040) | Sole proprietors and single-member LLCs |
| Form 1065 | Partnerships and multi-member LLCs (taxed as partnership) |
| Form 1120-S | S-Corporations |
| Form 1120 | C-Corporations |
| Form IT-20S (Indiana) | Indiana S-Corp return |
| Form IT-65 (Indiana) | Indiana partnership return |

**When business returns are due:**
- Partnerships (1065) and S-Corps (1120-S): March 15 (6-month extension available to September 15)
- C-Corps (1120): April 15 (6-month extension available to October 15)
- Extensions extend the filing deadline only — taxes owed are still due on the original date

## Tax Planning (Year-Round)

Tax preparation is looking backward; tax planning is looking forward. Planning clients meet with their CPA 1–3 times per year to:

- Estimate annual tax liability and adjust withholding or estimated payments
- Identify opportunities to accelerate deductions or defer income
- Review retirement contribution options (SEP-IRA, Solo 401k, SIMPLE IRA)
- Plan around major events: equipment purchases, hiring, real estate transactions, business sale
- Review entity structure as the business grows

Tax planning is available as a stand-alone service or included in our Business Advisory retainer packages.
