# Greenfield CPA — Individual Tax Preparation Checklist

Use this checklist to gather your documents before your tax prep appointment. You don't need every item — only what applies to your situation. Bring what you have; we'll let you know if something is missing.

---

## Personal Information

- [ ] Social Security numbers for you, spouse, and all dependents
- [ ] Dates of birth for all listed on the return
- [ ] Prior year tax return (Form 1040 + all schedules + state return) — the single most useful document you can bring
- [ ] Bank account and routing number for direct deposit of refund (or to pay any balance due)
- [ ] Identity Protection PIN (IP PIN) if you've been issued one by the IRS

---

## Income Documents

Employers and financial institutions are required to mail these by January 31.

**Employment:**
- [ ] W-2 from each employer (one per job)
- [ ] W-2G for any gambling winnings over $600

**Self-Employment / Freelance:**
- [ ] 1099-NEC from each client who paid you $600 or more
- [ ] 1099-K if you received payments through payment apps (Venmo, PayPal, Square) — now required for amounts over $5,000 in 2024
- [ ] Your own records of all business income (even amounts not on a 1099)
- [ ] Business expense records (see Schedule C section below)

**Investment Income:**
- [ ] 1099-INT for bank and savings account interest
- [ ] 1099-DIV for dividends from investments or mutual funds
- [ ] 1099-B for stock, mutual fund, or ETF sales — also bring cost basis records if not on the 1099-B
- [ ] 1099-S if you sold real estate
- [ ] K-1 if you have income from a partnership, S-Corp, estate, or trust

**Retirement & Government:**
- [ ] 1099-R for distributions from IRAs, 401(k)s, pensions, or annuities
- [ ] SSA-1099 for Social Security benefits received
- [ ] 1099-G for state tax refunds (only taxable if you itemized last year) and unemployment compensation

**Other Income:**
- [ ] 1099-MISC for miscellaneous income (royalties, rent paid to you as landlord under $600, etc.)
- [ ] Alimony received (if divorce finalized before 2019 — different rules apply)

---

## Deduction Documents

**Standard vs. Itemized:** Most people take the standard deduction ($14,600 single / $29,200 married filing jointly for 2024). Itemizing makes sense if your deductible expenses exceed these amounts. Bring these documents either way — we'll determine which is better for you.

**Home Ownership:**
- [ ] Form 1098 from your mortgage lender (shows mortgage interest paid)
- [ ] Property tax bills paid in 2024
- [ ] Settlement statement (HUD-1 or Closing Disclosure) if you bought or sold a home

**Charitable Contributions:**
- [ ] Receipts for cash donations over $250 (must have written acknowledgment from the charity)
- [ ] Records of non-cash donations (clothing, household goods donated to Goodwill, etc.) — fair market value receipts
- [ ] Mileage log if you drove for charitable purposes (14 cents/mile for 2024)

**Medical & Dental:**
- [ ] Total out-of-pocket medical expenses paid in 2024 (insurance premiums, co-pays, prescriptions, glasses, dental work)
- [ ] Only deductible to the extent they exceed 7.5% of AGI — worth calculating if you had significant medical costs

**Other Itemized Deductions:**
- [ ] Student loan interest (Form 1098-E) — deductible up to $2,500 even if you don't itemize
- [ ] State income taxes paid (capped at $10,000 combined with property taxes)

---

## Credits & Special Situations

**Children & Dependents:**
- [ ] Childcare provider name, address, and EIN/SSN (for Child and Dependent Care Credit)
- [ ] Form 1098-T from college/university (for education credits)
- [ ] Adoption expense records if you adopted a child in 2024

**Health Insurance:**
- [ ] Form 1095-A if you had marketplace (ACA) health insurance
- [ ] Form 1095-B or 1095-C if provided by employer (typically not needed but bring if you have it)

**Other:**
- [ ] IRS notices received in 2024 — bring copies of everything
- [ ] Estimated tax payments made (dates and amounts if not on a 1099)
- [ ] Foreign financial accounts or income (FBAR obligations may apply)
- [ ] Energy-efficient home improvement receipts (solar panels, heat pumps, insulation — may qualify for credits)

---

## Schedule C (Self-Employment / Business Income on Personal Return)

If you have freelance income, a side business, or are a sole proprietor, bring:
- [ ] Total gross income (all payments received, 1099 and non-1099)
- [ ] Records of business expenses by category:
  - Advertising and marketing
  - Home office (square footage, total home size, or actual expenses)
  - Vehicle (total miles driven for business, total annual miles, and date placed in service)
  - Travel, meals (50% deductible), and entertainment
  - Equipment and tools purchased
  - Software and subscriptions
  - Professional fees (legal, accounting)
  - Insurance premiums paid for the business
  - Subcontractor costs (with names and EINs if paid over $600)

---

## Questions?

If you're unsure whether something is deductible or relevant, bring it anyway. It's easier for us to tell you it doesn't apply than to reconstruct records later. When in doubt, bring it.
