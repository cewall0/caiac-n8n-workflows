# Greenfield CPA — New Client Intake Guide

## Welcome to Greenfield CPA

We're glad you're considering Greenfield CPA for your tax and financial needs. This guide covers what to expect when working with us, how to prepare for your first meeting, and what information we'll need to get started.

## What We Need to Know About You

At your initial consultation, we'll ask about your situation to determine the right services and preparer for you. The more information you can share upfront, the more productive your first meeting will be.

**For Individual Clients:**
- Filing status (single, married filing jointly, married filing separately, head of household)
- State(s) where you live and work — if these differ, you may have multi-state filing obligations
- Whether you own a home (mortgage interest, property taxes) or rent
- Whether you received any new income sources this year (new job, freelance work, rental income, investment sales, retirement distributions)
- Any significant life changes: marriage, divorce, new baby, child leaving as a dependent, home purchase or sale, business started or closed, inheritance received
- Whether you received any IRS or state notices — bring these, even if you think they're resolved

**For Business Clients:**
- Business entity type: Sole proprietor, LLC (single-member or multi-member), S-Corp, C-Corp, Partnership
- State of formation and states where the business operates
- Fiscal year (calendar year vs. non-calendar year)
- Payroll: do you have employees or contractors? Do you use a payroll service?
- Prior year business returns (at least 2 years if possible)
- Whether you want bookkeeping services in addition to tax preparation

## Your First Meeting

Initial consultations are typically 30–60 minutes. We can meet in-person at our office (4218 Commerce Drive, Suite 200, Carmel, IN 46032), by phone, or by video call — your choice.

**What to bring:**
- A copy of last year's tax return (the most important document — it tells us everything about your prior situation)
- A photo ID
- Your Social Security number (and your spouse's, if filing jointly)
- Social Security numbers for any dependents
- Business entity documents if you're a new business client (EIN letter from IRS, operating agreement, articles of incorporation)

You do not need to bring all of your income documents to the first meeting — that's for the preparation meeting. The initial consultation is about understanding your situation and making sure we're the right fit.

## Our Engagement Letter

Before we begin work, we ask every client to sign an engagement letter. This document:
- Describes the specific services we're providing (e.g., "Preparation of 2024 Form 1040, Indiana IT-40, and Schedule C")
- States our fees and billing terms
- Clarifies that you are responsible for the accuracy and completeness of information you provide to us
- Explains our confidentiality obligations

We do not begin work until the engagement letter is signed and, for new clients, an initial payment is received.

## Our Fee Structure

We charge on a per-form basis for most individual tax returns. Fees vary based on complexity.

**Individual returns (approximate ranges):**
| Return Type | Fee Range |
|---|---|
| Simple 1040 (W-2 income only, standard deduction) | $200–$350 |
| 1040 with itemized deductions | $350–$550 |
| 1040 with Schedule C (self-employment) | $450–$750 |
| 1040 with rental property (Schedule E) | $500–$800 |
| 1040 with investment sales (Schedule D / Form 8949) | $400–$700 |
| Multi-state returns | Add $150–$250 per additional state |

**Business returns (approximate ranges):**
| Return Type | Fee Range |
|---|---|
| Schedule C (simple sole prop) | $200–$400 |
| Form 1065 (Partnership) | $800–$2,500 |
| Form 1120-S (S-Corp) | $900–$2,500 |
| Form 1120 (C-Corp) | $1,200–$4,000 |

Bookkeeping and payroll services are priced separately on a monthly retainer basis.

## Confidentiality

Everything you share with Greenfield CPA is confidential. We do not share your information with third parties without your written consent, except as required by law. Our team members are bound by professional ethics standards under Indiana CPA licensing.
