# Buyer & Seller FAQ — Hargrove Realty Group

## Buyer Questions

**How long does the home buying process take?**
From starting your search to closing, most buyers take 2–4 months. The timeline depends on the market and how quickly you find the right home. Once an offer is accepted, closing typically takes 30–45 days.

**Do I need to be pre-approved before I start looking?**
We strongly recommend it. Sellers won't accept offers from buyers who aren't pre-approved, and in a competitive market, homes can go under contract within hours of listing. Getting pre-approved first means you're ready to move when you find the right home.

**How much do I need for a down payment?**
It depends on your loan type. Conventional loans start at 3–5% down (though 20% avoids private mortgage insurance). FHA loans require 3.5% down. VA and USDA loans may require zero down for qualifying buyers. Your lender will walk through the best option for your situation.

**What are closing costs?**
Closing costs are fees paid at the closing table to complete the transaction. Buyers typically pay 2–5% of the loan amount in closing costs, which include lender fees, title insurance, prepaid taxes and insurance, and recording fees. Your lender will give you a Loan Estimate within 3 business days of application, which details expected costs.

**Can I back out of a purchase after my offer is accepted?**
Yes, within your contingency windows — typically inspection (7–10 days) and financing. If you back out for a covered reason, your earnest money is returned. If you back out outside of contingencies without cause, you may forfeit your earnest money deposit.

**What's included in a home inspection?**
A licensed inspector examines the home's major systems and components: foundation, framing, roof, HVAC, plumbing, electrical, insulation, windows, and doors. They won't inspect inside walls or test every outlet but will identify visible defects. Inspections typically take 2–3 hours and cost $300–$500 depending on home size.

**What if the home appraises below the purchase price?**
You have three options: negotiate a price reduction with the seller, pay the difference in cash out of pocket, or exit under the appraisal contingency with your earnest money returned. We'll advise you on the best approach based on the specifics.

**What does the buyer's agent commission cost me?**
In most transactions, the seller pays the buyer's agent fee. Following the 2024 NAR settlement, we'll provide a clear written disclosure of compensation before you sign a Buyer Agency Agreement. We'll walk through the specifics at your consultation.

## Seller Questions

**How do you determine the listing price?**
We prepare a Comparative Market Analysis (CMA) — a detailed review of recently sold homes similar to yours in size, condition, location, and features. We present a recommended price range and explain the data behind it. The final decision is yours.

**How long will it take to sell my home?**
It depends on price, condition, and market conditions. Correctly priced, well-prepared homes in good markets often go under contract within days. Overpriced homes can sit for months and ultimately sell for less. We'll tell you honestly what to expect before we list.

**What do I need to do to prepare my home for sale?**
At minimum: deep clean, declutter, and address any obvious deferred maintenance. We'll do a walkthrough before listing and give you specific recommendations. Professional photography is included in our listing service. Major renovations rarely pay for themselves — we'll tell you which improvements are worth making and which aren't.

**Do I have to accept the highest offer?**
No. You can evaluate offers based on price, contingencies, financing strength, and timeline. A lower cash offer with no contingencies may be preferable to a higher financed offer with more risk. We'll help you compare the net outcome of each offer, not just the headline number.

**What if a buyer requests repairs after the inspection?**
You have options: make the repairs, offer a credit at closing, reduce the price, or negotiate a combination. You're not obligated to fix everything. We'll advise you on what's reasonable and what's not worth conceding.

**When do I receive my proceeds?**
Sellers typically receive net proceeds by wire transfer the same day closing documents are recorded, or the following business day. Your net proceeds equal the sale price minus your mortgage payoff, commission, closing costs, and any agreed-upon credits to the buyer.

**What if my home doesn't sell?**
If we don't receive acceptable offers, we'll have a candid conversation about why — usually pricing, condition, or market conditions — and recommend adjustments. We'd rather give you honest advice than keep a listing going in the wrong direction.

**Can I sell while I'm still paying a mortgage?**
Yes. Your mortgage is paid off at closing from your sale proceeds. You don't need to pay it off before listing. We'll show you your estimated net proceeds before you commit to listing.
