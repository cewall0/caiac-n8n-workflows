# Seller's Guide — Hargrove Realty Group

## How We Price Your Home

Pricing your home correctly from the start is the single most important factor in getting a strong sale. Overpriced homes sit on the market, go stale, and eventually sell for less than they would have if priced right initially. We price based on a Comparative Market Analysis (CMA) — a detailed review of recently sold homes in your neighborhood that are similar in size, condition, age, and features.

We look at:
- **Sold comparables** — homes that closed in the last 90 days within a half-mile radius
- **Active competition** — what buyers are choosing between right now
- **Market trend** — whether values are rising, flat, or softening
- **Your home's condition and updates** — upgrades that add value vs. deferred maintenance that reduces it

We'll present a recommended price range and explain exactly how we arrived at it. The final price is your decision — we'll give you our honest professional opinion.

## Preparing Your Home

Buyers decide within minutes whether they're interested. Presentation matters. Before we list, we'll walk the property with you and recommend which improvements are worth making. Generally:

**Almost always worth it:**
- Deep cleaning, including windows, baseboards, and appliances
- Decluttering and removing personal items
- Fresh neutral paint on walls that are marked, dated, or bold
- Landscaping cleanup — mow, edge, mulch, trim overgrowth
- Fixing obvious defects: dripping faucets, broken fixtures, squeaky doors

**Evaluate case by case:**
- New flooring (depends on current condition and price point)
- Kitchen or bath updates (only if significantly dated and the numbers work)
- Major repairs (we'll advise on whether to fix or disclose-and-price-accordingly)

We coordinate professional photography for every listing — it's included in our service. Homes with professional photos sell faster and for more. We may also recommend a 3D virtual tour, especially for higher price points.

## Showings and Offers

Once listed, be ready to accommodate showings on short notice — buyers often decide quickly. The easier your home is to show, the more buyers will see it. We'll use a lockbox and scheduling system that lets agents book showings directly while notifying you of each appointment.

When offers arrive, we'll present all of them and help you evaluate:
- **Net proceeds** — not just price, but concessions, closing cost contributions, and contingencies
- **Financing strength** — pre-approved buyers vs. cash vs. uncertain financing
- **Contingencies** — inspection, appraisal, and financing contingencies affect your risk
- **Timeline** — closing date and any possession requests

You can accept, reject, or counter any offer. In a multiple-offer situation, we'll advise you on how to run it — whether to accept the best offer, counter one buyer, or call for highest and best.

## From Accepted Offer to Closing

Once you accept an offer, the buyer will schedule a home inspection within 7–10 days. If they request repairs, we'll negotiate to reach an agreement that protects your proceeds. You're not obligated to fix everything — credits and price adjustments are often the cleaner solution.

The buyer's lender will order an appraisal. If the home appraises below the purchase price, we'll help you navigate the conversation — whether that's renegotiating, holding firm, or moving to backup offers.

At closing, you'll sign the deed and transfer documents (usually 30–60 minutes), and proceeds will be wired to your account the same day or next business day. Our commission is paid at closing from your proceeds — no upfront costs.
