# Apex Roofing — Storm Damage & Insurance Claims Guide

## After a Hail or Wind Storm — What to Do

Indiana sits in one of the most active hail corridors in the country. A significant hail event (stones 1" diameter or larger) can damage or destroy a roof even when it looks fine from the ground. Here's what to do after a storm.

### Immediate Steps

1. **Safety first:** Do not walk on a wet or damaged roof. If you see active water intrusion inside your home, place buckets and call us for emergency tarping.
2. **Look for obvious exterior damage:** Check gutters (dents and granule accumulation), downspouts, AC unit fins, painted wood trim, and window screens. Hail that damages these surfaces almost certainly damaged your roof.
3. **Do not make permanent repairs yet:** Making repairs before an insurance inspection could complicate your claim. Temporary tarping is fine; permanent patching is not.
4. **Call Apex for a free inspection:** We document hail and wind damage thoroughly and provide a written assessment you can submit to your insurer.

## Understanding Hail Damage

Hail damage to asphalt shingles appears as:
- **Impact bruises** — soft spots where the granule layer was knocked off; the underlying asphalt mat is exposed or cracked
- **Granule loss** — visible bare spots or a significant accumulation of granules in gutters after a storm
- **Circular impact marks** — visible from the roof surface on the shingles, typically in a random pattern (compared to mechanical damage, which follows a pattern)

One storm can add years of wear to a 15-year-old shingle or cause damage that immediately compromises a brand-new roof's performance. Only a trained eye on the roof surface can assess the extent.

## How Insurance Claims Work for Roofing

### Step 1 — Apex Inspects First

Before calling your insurance company, let us inspect. We document:
- Hail size (cross-referenced with weather data for your address)
- Number and location of impact points per 100 sq ft (adjusters use thresholds to determine if replacement is warranted — typically 8–10+ hits per 10×10 square)
- Damage to other exterior components (gutters, siding, trim)
- Photos of all damage points

We provide this documentation in a format your insurance adjuster expects.

### Step 2 — File Your Claim

After our inspection confirms damage, file a claim with your homeowners insurer. You'll be assigned a claim number and an adjuster.

**What to tell your insurer:**
- Date of the storm (we can provide this from weather data if you're unsure)
- That you've had a professional roof inspection
- That you'd like an adjuster to inspect the roof

Most Indiana insurers schedule adjuster visits within 5–10 business days.

### Step 3 — Adjuster Inspection

We strongly recommend having an Apex representative present during the adjuster's inspection. Our estimator can:
- Walk the adjuster through the damage we documented
- Ensure all damage points are included in the adjuster's scope
- Clarify any points the adjuster questions
- Advocate for complete replacement vs. partial repair when warranted

Having a contractor present typically results in a more complete scope. We do this at no additional charge.

### Step 4 — Review the Scope of Loss

Your insurer will send a written Scope of Loss document detailing what they're approving and the payout amount. Review it carefully:
- Confirm the estimate includes complete tear-off and replacement (not just surface repair)
- Check for replacement cost value (RCV) vs. actual cash value (ACV) — RCV pays replacement cost; ACV deducts depreciation
- Check for "recoverable depreciation" — if your policy includes RCV, the insurer initially pays ACV, then releases the held-back depreciation after repairs are completed

If the scope seems low, do not sign anything. Contact Apex — we can negotiate supplements or, if necessary, connect you with a public adjuster.

### Step 5 — Schedule Your Replacement

Once the claim is approved and you've reviewed the scope:
- Sign the Apex contract
- We handle all permits (Hamilton and Marion County permits typically take 5–7 business days)
- Project is scheduled; materials ordered
- Installation completed per our standard process

**We work directly with your insurer** for supplemental payments (for items initially missed by the adjuster) and final payment coordination. You should never have to chase your insurer for payment related to a roofing claim we're managing.

## What Insurance Typically Covers

- Complete tear-off and disposal of damaged roofing
- Replacement with materials of like kind and quality
- Code upgrade costs (replacing to current building code — e.g., adding ice and water shield if not present) — if you have Ordinance or Law coverage
- Related damage to gutters, fascia, and siding in the same storm

**What insurance does NOT cover:**
- Pre-existing damage (worn-out shingles, prior leaks)
- Cosmetic damage only (scuffs or marks that don't affect function) on newer roofs, per some policies
- Roof replacement if your roof is older than the policy's functional age threshold (some policies reduce or deny claims on roofs over 20 years old)

## Red Flags to Watch For

Storm chasers (out-of-state contractors who flood an area after a major storm) often pressure homeowners to sign Assignment of Benefits forms. **Do not sign an AOB.** It transfers your claim rights to the contractor and removes your ability to manage your own claim. Apex never asks for AOB.

Choose a local, licensed Indiana contractor with a permanent office and verifiable history. We've been in business in Indiana since 2008.
