# Apex Roofing — Roofing Materials Guide

## Choosing the Right Roofing Material

The right material depends on your budget, the look you want, your roof's pitch, and how long you plan to stay in the home. Here's an honest breakdown of every major option we install.

---

## Asphalt Shingles

By far the most common residential roofing material in the Midwest. Cost-effective, widely available, and easy to repair.

### 3-Tab Shingles
The traditional "flat" shingle — a single layer with uniform slots that give the appearance of three separate shingles per strip. Less common than they used to be; being phased out in favor of architectural.

- **Lifespan:** 15–20 years
- **Cost range:** $3.50–$5.00 per square foot installed (materials + labor)
- **Wind rating:** Up to 60 mph
- **Pros:** Lowest upfront cost; simple installation
- **Cons:** Shorter lifespan, lower wind resistance, flat appearance, difficult to insure as new installs with some carriers

### Architectural (Dimensional) Shingles
The current standard. Two layers laminated together create a dimensional look that mimics wood shake or slate. Significantly better performance than 3-tab.

- **Lifespan:** 25–30 years (manufacturer warranty); 25–40 years real-world
- **Cost range:** $4.50–$7.00 per square foot installed
- **Wind rating:** 110–130 mph (Class 4 impact-resistant upgrades available)
- **Pros:** Better aesthetics, longer life, stronger wind/hail resistance, widely available in dozens of colors
- **Cons:** More expensive than 3-tab, still not as long-lasting as metal or tile

**Apex recommendation:** Architectural shingles are the right choice for most Indiana homeowners — the best balance of cost, performance, and curb appeal.

### Class 4 Impact-Resistant Shingles
A premium version of architectural shingles with a rubberized asphalt core that passes the UL 2218 Class 4 impact test. These shingles are specifically designed to resist hail damage.

- **Lifespan:** 30+ years
- **Cost range:** $6.00–$9.00 per square foot installed (20–30% premium over standard architectural)
- **Insurance savings:** Many Indiana insurers (including State Farm, Allstate) offer 15–30% discounts on homeowners premiums for Class 4 roofs — often offsetting the cost premium within 3–5 years
- **Apex recommendation:** Strongly recommended for homes in hail-prone areas. Ask us about the insurance discount documentation we provide.

## Metal Roofing

Growing rapidly in residential applications. Initially more expensive but significantly longer-lived.

### Standing Seam Metal
Concealed fasteners and raised seams create a clean, modern look. The premium metal option.

- **Lifespan:** 40–70 years
- **Cost range:** $10.00–$18.00 per square foot installed
- **Wind rating:** Up to 140 mph
- **Pros:** Longest lifespan of any mainstream option, virtually maintenance-free, excellent for solar panel installation, fire-resistant
- **Cons:** Significant upfront cost, visible oil-canning (waviness) on some panels, requires experienced installer (Apex is certified)

### Exposed Fastener Metal (R-Panel / Corrugated)
More affordable metal option using exposed screws. Common on agricultural buildings but used residentially for modern/farmhouse aesthetics.

- **Lifespan:** 25–40 years
- **Cost range:** $5.50–$9.00 per square foot installed
- **Pros:** Lower cost than standing seam, durable, modern look
- **Cons:** Exposed fasteners can fail over time and require periodic inspection

---

## Tile Roofing

### Concrete Tile
Excellent durability, popular in Spanish/Mediterranean-style homes.

- **Lifespan:** 40–50 years
- **Cost range:** $8.00–$13.00 per square foot installed
- **Pros:** Long life, fire-resistant, excellent hail performance
- **Cons:** Heavy (requires structural assessment), higher installation complexity, higher cost

---

## Flat Roofing — TPO Membrane

For low-slope roofs (less than 2:12 pitch), traditional shingles don't work. TPO (Thermoplastic Polyolefin) is the modern standard.

- **Lifespan:** 15–30 years depending on thickness and installation quality
- **Cost range:** $5.00–$8.00 per square foot installed
- **Pros:** Energy-efficient (white surface reflects heat), seam-welded (very water-tight), works on flat and low-slope roofs
- **Cons:** Puncture risk in high-traffic roof areas, requires qualified installer
- **Apex installs:** 60 mil TPO minimum (do not accept less from any contractor)
