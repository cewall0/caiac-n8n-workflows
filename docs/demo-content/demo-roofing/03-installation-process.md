# Apex Roofing — Our Installation Process

## What to Expect From Start to Finish

A roof replacement is a significant project. We walk every homeowner through exactly what's going to happen so there are no surprises. Here's the full process from first contact to final inspection.

---

## Step 1: Free Roof Inspection

We start every project with a no-obligation inspection. An Apex estimator will:
- Walk the roof and inspect all surfaces, ridgelines, valleys, and penetrations
- Check flashing at chimneys, vents, skylights, and walls
- Inspect the attic for ventilation, decking condition, and signs of leaks
- Check gutters and fascia for related damage
- Document everything with photos

The inspection takes 45–90 minutes depending on roof size and complexity. We'll explain what we find in plain language, not roofing jargon.

**Scheduling:** Call (317) 555-0190 or book online at apexroofingindiana.com. Inspections are typically available within 3–5 business days. After a major storm, wait times may be longer as we prioritize emergency tarping and safety inspections.

---

## Step 2: Estimate & Contract

After the inspection, we prepare a detailed written estimate. This includes:
- Material specifications (manufacturer, product line, color options)
- Scope of work (number of layers being removed, decking repairs if needed, flashing replacement)
- Labor and material costs itemized
- Project timeline
- Payment schedule

We do not pressure for same-day signatures. Review the estimate, ask questions, compare if you'd like. When you're ready to proceed, we collect a deposit (typically 10–25% of the project total, see Pricing Guide) and schedule your project.

---

## Step 3: Material Delivery (1–2 Days Before)

Roofing materials are delivered directly to your property 1–2 days before the project start date. The delivery truck uses a boom arm to place pallets of shingles, underlayment, ice and water shield, and other materials on your driveway or directly on the roof if access allows.

**What to do:**
- Clear your driveway of vehicles if possible
- Let neighbors know (it's noisy — we deliver early morning)
- Keep children and pets away from the material staging area

---

## Step 4: Day of Installation

**Start time:** Our crews typically arrive between 7:00–8:00 AM. We work in a single continuous day when weather permits. Most standard residential roofs (1,500–2,500 sq ft) are completed in 1 day. Larger roofs and complex roofs with multiple levels or many penetrations may take 2 days.

**The process:**
1. **Protect your property:** Tarps are placed over landscaping, AC units, and around the perimeter to catch falling debris
2. **Tear-off:** Old roofing layers are stripped and thrown directly into a dump trailer (parked in your driveway — we handle all disposal)
3. **Deck inspection:** With the old material off, we inspect the plywood decking for rot, delamination, or damage. Repairs are made before new material goes on. Any deck repairs beyond normal scope are communicated to you before proceeding, with pricing.
4. **Underlayment:** Ice and water shield is applied in the first 3 feet from eaves and in all valleys. Synthetic felt underlayment covers the remaining deck.
5. **Starter course:** A pre-cut starter strip is installed along eaves and rakes.
6. **Shingles:** Installed from the bottom up, nailed according to manufacturer specs. Each shingle gets 6 nails (we do not use 4-nail patterns — this matters for wind ratings and warranty).
7. **Flashing:** Step flashing, counter flashing, and pipe boot flashing are installed or replaced as part of every job. We do not leave original flashing in place unless it is in excellent condition and you explicitly choose that option.
8. **Ridge:** Ventilated ridge cap is installed along the peak.
9. **Cleanup:** Magnetic sweepers are run over all lawn areas to pick up nails. Debris is removed in the dump trailer. Final walkthrough with the homeowner.

---

## Step 5: Final Walkthrough & Documentation

Before we leave, an Apex supervisor walks the completed roof with you (from ground level) and discusses:
- What was done
- Any deck repairs made and why
- Material warranty registration (we handle this)
- Workmanship warranty certificate
- What to watch for in the first season

We also provide before/after photos of the project.

## A Typical Project Timeline

| Phase | Timeline |
|---|---|
| Initial inspection | Day 1 |
| Estimate delivered | Within 1 business day |
| Contract signed → project scheduled | 2–6 weeks (depends on season; summer is busy) |
| Material delivery | 1–2 days before project |
| Installation | 1–2 days |
| Final documentation | Same day as installation |

**Busy season (May–September):** Lead time for non-emergency projects is typically 3–6 weeks. Storm season (large hail events) extends lead times — we prioritize active leaks and emergency tarping.

**Off-season (October–March):** Lead times shrink to 1–2 weeks. We install year-round in Indiana. Asphalt shingles can be installed at temperatures above 40°F. We monitor weather and reschedule if necessary — no installation happens in rain or snow.
