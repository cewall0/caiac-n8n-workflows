# Mountain State Insurance — Claims Process

## When to File a Claim

File a claim whenever you experience a covered loss — a car accident, storm damage to your home, theft, or liability incident. Don't assume a loss is too small to claim; call us and we'll help you decide whether filing makes sense given your deductible and potential premium impact.

**When NOT to file:** If the damage is less than or close to your deductible, it may not make sense financially. A $600 repair with a $500 deductible results in a $100 payout but a claim on your record that could affect your premium for 3–5 years. Call us and we'll walk through the math with you before you decide.

## How to Report a Claim

**Option 1 — 24/7 Claims Line:** Call (812) 555-0340, Option 2. Available around the clock, including nights and weekends. For emergencies (total vehicle loss, home uninhabitable, injury accident), always call this line first.

**Option 2 — Online:** Submit a claim through our client portal at mountainstateins.com/claims. Upload photos and documents directly.

**Option 3 — Your Agent:** Call or email your Mountain State agent directly during business hours. We can initiate the claim on your behalf.

**What you'll need:**
- Your policy number (on your declarations page or insurance card)
- Date, time, and location of the incident
- Description of what happened
- Police report number (if applicable — always file a police report for vehicle theft, hit-and-run, and significant accidents)
- Contact information for other parties involved (auto claims)
- Photos of damage

## What Happens After You Report

**Step 1 — Claim Assignment (within 1 business day)**
Your claim is assigned to a claims adjuster who will be your point of contact throughout the process. You'll receive a claim number and the adjuster's direct contact information by email or text.

**Step 2 — Investigation & Inspection (2–5 business days)**
The adjuster reviews your claim and may schedule an inspection of the damage. For auto claims, you'll typically visit an approved repair shop or a mobile estimator will come to you. For property claims, the adjuster may visit your home or use your submitted photos for smaller claims.

**Step 3 — Coverage Determination (within 10 business days of inspection)**
The adjuster determines whether the loss is covered under your policy and calculates the claim payout. If additional information is needed, they'll contact you. You'll receive a written coverage determination.

**Step 4 — Payment**
- **Auto:** Payment goes directly to the repair shop if you use one of our preferred shops, or to you if you're arranging repairs independently.
- **Property:** For smaller claims, a check is issued to you. For larger claims (typically over $10,000), payment is co-payable to you and your mortgage lender.
- **Timeline:** Most straightforward claims are paid within 15–30 business days of the initial report.

## After an Auto Accident — Immediate Steps

1. **Safety first:** Move vehicles out of traffic if possible. Check for injuries.
2. **Call 911:** For any accident with injuries, always call police. For minor accidents, a police report is still recommended.
3. **Document the scene:** Take photos of all vehicles, damage, license plates, driver's licenses, and insurance cards from all parties.
4. **Do not admit fault** at the scene — even saying "I'm sorry" can be used against you.
5. **Report to Mountain State** as soon as possible — ideally the same day.

## After a Home Loss

1. **Ensure safety:** Evacuate if necessary. Do not enter a structurally damaged home.
2. **Prevent further damage:** You have a duty to prevent additional loss after a covered event. Cover broken windows, put a tarp over a damaged roof section. Keep receipts for any emergency protective measures — these costs are reimbursable.
3. **Document everything:** Take photos and video before any cleanup. Make a list of damaged or destroyed items.
4. **Report to Mountain State** within 24–48 hours of the loss.

## Dispute Resolution

If you disagree with the claims outcome, you have options:
1. Request a supervisor review through our claims department
2. Invoke the appraisal process (available in property claims) — each party selects an appraiser, and a neutral umpire resolves disputes
3. File a complaint with the Indiana Department of Insurance: idoi.in.gov, (317) 232-2385

We always aim to resolve claims fairly. If you feel your claim was handled incorrectly, please reach out — we want to get it right.
