# Mountain State Insurance — Policy Endorsements & Add-Ons

## What Is an Endorsement?

An endorsement (also called a rider or floater) is an amendment to your standard policy that adds, removes, or modifies coverage. Endorsements are the way to customize your policy for your specific situation. Most cost $25–$200/year and can prevent thousands of dollars in unpleasant surprises.

## Homeowners Endorsements

### Water Backup & Sump Pump Overflow
Standard homeowners policies exclude water damage that enters through a sewer backup or a failed sump pump. In Indiana's climate, this is one of the most common — and most expensive — homeowners claims. A finished basement can easily exceed $20,000 in damage.

**Cost:** $50–$100/year
**Coverage:** Typically $5,000–$25,000 (can be increased)
**Recommendation:** This is the #1 endorsement we recommend to Indiana homeowners. Almost everyone should have it.

### Scheduled Personal Property (Floater)
Standard homeowners policies have sub-limits on high-value items:
- Jewelry: $1,500 (theft) to $2,500 (all perils) — typical limit
- Firearms: $2,500
- Silverware: $2,500
- Art and collectibles: $2,500
- Musical instruments: $2,500

If you own items that exceed these limits, you need a Personal Property Floater. Items are scheduled individually with an agreed value (usually based on a recent appraisal or receipt). The floater covers loss, theft, mysterious disappearance (you lose it and don't know where), and accidental damage.

**Cost:** Typically 1–2% of the scheduled item's value per year. A $10,000 engagement ring costs approximately $100–$200/year to schedule.
**Recommendation:** Always schedule engagement rings, fine jewelry, watches, and any item worth more than the policy sub-limit.

### Home Business Endorsement
If you run any business activity from your home — including working from home on company equipment, storing business inventory, or seeing clients — your standard homeowners policy may exclude it. This endorsement adds coverage for business equipment and limited liability for business activities.

**Cost:** $25–$75/year for basic coverage
**Note:** For a full home-based business (retail, professional services with clients on-site, significant inventory), a separate business owner's policy (BOP) may be more appropriate.

### Equipment Breakdown
Covers the cost to repair or replace household systems and appliances when they break down due to mechanical or electrical failure — not covered by standard homeowners policies, which only cover sudden perils (fire, storm, etc.).

**Covered:** HVAC systems, water heaters, electrical panels, refrigerators, washers/dryers, computers, smart home systems
**Cost:** $30–$60/year
**Recommendation:** Worth considering for homes with newer smart home systems or appliances still under the original cost.

### Ordinance or Law
If your home is damaged and local building codes have changed since it was built, you may be required to bring non-damaged portions up to current code during repairs. Standard policies only pay to restore to the original condition — this endorsement covers the cost of code compliance upgrades.

**Cost:** $50–$150/year
**Recommendation:** Especially valuable for homes built before 1990.

## Umbrella Policy

A personal umbrella policy provides liability coverage above and beyond your homeowners and auto policies. If you're sued after a serious accident and the judgment exceeds your underlying policy limits, your umbrella kicks in.

**Coverage options:** $1 million, $2 million, $3 million, $5 million
**Cost:** $150–$300/year for $1 million; $75–$100 for each additional million
**Who needs it:** Homeowners with significant assets, anyone with a pool or trampoline, dog owners, landlords, frequent hosts, or anyone with a teen driver. It's the most cost-effective liability coverage available.

**Requirement:** You must carry minimum underlying liability limits on your auto ($250,000/$500,000) and homeowners ($300,000) policies to qualify.

## Auto Endorsements

### Gap Insurance
Pays the difference between what your car is worth (actual cash value) and what you owe on your loan if the car is totaled. Essential for new car purchases in the first 2–3 years when depreciation outpaces your loan payoff.

**Cost:** $20–$40/year (much less expensive than dealer-offered gap through the lender)

### New Car Replacement
For brand new vehicles (under 1 year old with under 15,000 miles), pays to replace your car with a brand new vehicle of the same make and model rather than paying actual cash value.

**Cost:** $50–$100/year for the first year; typically drops off after year two

### Rideshare Coverage
If you drive for Uber or Lyft, your personal auto policy does not cover you while you're logged into the app waiting for a ride request. This endorsement fills that gap. Rideshare companies provide coverage once a ride is accepted, but there's a window where you're exposed.

**Cost:** $10–$20/month
