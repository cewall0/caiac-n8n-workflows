# Mountain State Insurance — Deductibles, Premiums & Discounts

## What Is a Deductible?

A deductible is the amount you pay out-of-pocket before your insurance coverage kicks in. If you have a $1,000 deductible and file a $6,000 claim, you pay $1,000 and insurance pays $5,000.

**Deductibles apply per claim, not per year** (unlike health insurance deductibles). Every claim has its own deductible.

**Choosing a deductible:** Higher deductibles mean lower monthly/annual premiums — you're accepting more risk in exchange for lower cost. This is a smart tradeoff if you have an emergency fund and rarely file claims. Lower deductibles mean higher premiums but less out-of-pocket when something goes wrong.

**Homeowners deductible options:** $500, $1,000, $1,500, $2,000, $2,500, $5,000
**Auto deductible options (collision/comprehensive):** $250, $500, $1,000, $1,500, $2,000

## Wind/Hail Deductibles — Homeowners

In Indiana and many Midwest states, some policies carry a separate — and typically higher — deductible for wind and hail claims. This is separate from your standard deductible.

**Common structures:**
- Flat dollar amount ($2,000, $5,000) — you pay this specific amount regardless of claim size
- Percentage-based (1%–5% of dwelling coverage) — e.g., 2% of a $300,000 dwelling = $6,000 deductible for that claim

Check your declarations page carefully. Wind/hail damage is one of the most common homeowners claims in Indiana, and many policyholders are surprised by this deductible when they file.

## How Premiums Are Set

Your annual premium is calculated based on risk factors specific to your situation:

**Homeowners factors:**
- Home age, construction type (brick vs. wood frame), and square footage
- Replacement cost value
- Roof age and material (new roofs save 10–20%; roofs over 20 years old cost more or may be excluded for cosmetic hail damage)
- Proximity to a fire station and hydrant
- Claims history (both yours and the property's)
- Credit-based insurance score
- Location (flood zone, hail belt, crime rates)
- Security features (smoke alarms, security system, deadbolts)

**Auto factors:**
- Driving record and years of experience
- Vehicle make, model, year, and value
- Annual mileage
- Where the vehicle is garaged (ZIP code)
- Coverage levels and deductibles
- Credit-based insurance score
- Age and household members on the policy

## Premium Review — When to Check In With Us

Your premium is recalculated at each renewal. It's worth calling us to review your coverage if:
- You've made significant home improvements
- Your roof was replaced
- You paid off your car or bought a new one
- Your teenager started driving (rates change dramatically)
- Your teenager left for college (may reduce premium)
- You've been claim-free for 5+ years
- You bundled or unbundled home and auto

We also recommend a full coverage review every 3 years to ensure your dwelling coverage keeps pace with rising construction costs. A home insured for $250,000 five years ago may cost $320,000 to rebuild today.
