# Mountain State Insurance — Auto Insurance Guide

## Required vs. Optional Coverage

Indiana requires all drivers to carry minimum liability coverage. Most drivers benefit from carrying more than the state minimum.

**Indiana Minimum Requirements:**
- Bodily injury liability: $25,000 per person / $50,000 per accident
- Property damage liability: $25,000 per accident

These minimums are low. A serious accident can easily exceed them, leaving you personally liable for the balance. Mountain State recommends at least $100,000/$300,000 bodily injury and $100,000 property damage for most drivers.

## Coverage Types Explained

### Liability Coverage
Pays for injuries and property damage you cause to others in an accident where you are at fault. Does NOT cover your own vehicle or your own injuries.

- **Bodily injury liability:** Pays the other driver's medical bills, lost wages, and pain/suffering claims
- **Property damage liability:** Pays to repair or replace the other driver's vehicle and any other property you damage (fence, mailbox, building)

### Collision Coverage
Pays to repair or replace your own vehicle after a collision — whether you hit another car, a guardrail, or a pothole. Required by most lenders if you have a car loan or lease.

**Deductible:** You choose a deductible ($250, $500, $1,000, $1,500, $2,000). Higher deductible = lower premium. For older vehicles worth less than $4,000, dropping collision may make financial sense — ask your agent.

### Comprehensive Coverage
Pays for damage to your vehicle from non-collision events:
- Theft or attempted theft
- Hail, wind, or flood damage
- Fire
- Hitting an animal (deer, etc.)
- Falling objects (tree limb)
- Vandalism

Also uses your chosen deductible. Comprehensive and collision are typically sold together.

### Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist (UM/UIM)
One in eight Indiana drivers is uninsured. UM/UIM coverage pays your medical bills and vehicle repair costs if you're hit by a driver with no insurance or insufficient insurance to cover your damages.

Highly recommended — this is one of the best values in auto insurance. We recommend matching your UM limits to your liability limits.

### Medical Payments (MedPay)
Pays medical bills for you and your passengers, regardless of fault. Covers co-pays, deductibles, and expenses your health insurance doesn't cover. Available in $1,000–$10,000 increments.

### Rental Reimbursement
Pays for a rental car while your vehicle is being repaired after a covered claim. Typical coverage is $30–$50/day for up to 30 days. This is inexpensive to add (~$4/month) and very convenient.

### Roadside Assistance
Covers towing, flat tire change, jump starts, and lockout service. Available as an add-on if you don't have coverage through AAA or a manufacturer warranty.

### Gap Insurance
If you owe more on your vehicle loan than the car is worth (common in the first 1–3 years of ownership), gap insurance pays the difference between your insurance payout (actual cash value) and what you owe the lender. Important for new car purchases with small down payments.

## Factors That Affect Your Premium

- **Driving record:** At-fault accidents and moving violations (speeding, DUI) significantly increase premiums
- **Vehicle make/model/year:** Newer and more expensive vehicles cost more to insure; some models have higher theft or repair rates
- **Annual mileage:** Less time on the road = lower risk
- **Credit score:** In Indiana, insurers may use credit-based insurance scores as a rating factor
- **Age and experience:** Young drivers (under 25) typically pay more; experienced drivers over 25 with clean records pay less
- **Coverage and deductible choices:** Higher deductibles and lower limits reduce premiums
- **Location:** Urban areas with higher theft and accident rates cost more than rural areas
